MELONCHOLY

FOOD WASTE

Much of the food we grow rots in the field, and much of the food that's harvested is never even eaten. Consumers demand exceptionally perfect-looking produce, while discarding any items with even minor imperfections.

At the same time, hundreds of millions of people around the world and in the United States are going hungry. This is enough to make anyone feel upset -- maybe even a bit meloncholy.

But we can make a difference. Merced is located in the middle of one of the most productive farming areas in the world. If change is going to happen, it needs to start with us.

STATISTICS

80% of California's water is used for agriculture.

40% of food produced in America is thrown away.

Per capita food waste has increased 50% since 1974.

Converting edible crops into animal feed wastes 99% of the crops' energy.

10% of the Earth's surface is used to produce food that is wasted.

HOW TO HELP

Lower your standards: purchase foods that have superficial defects or blemishes -- the contents are still just as healthy.

Follow your nose: ignore expiration dates (they're arbitrary and misleading) -- use your nose to determine whether food is still safe to eat.

Go Freegan: buy discount food items, dumpster dive, and don't feel bad rescuing perfectly good food from the trash.

Screw the rules!: raid local farm fields after harvest -- the remaining foods are just waiting around to rot.

Save a cow: the only thing worse than throwing food away is feeding it to animals -- livestock produce more than half of global CO2 emissions.

Fight back: France recently passed a law making it illegal for supermarkets to throw away edible food -- we can do the same thing in Merced!
